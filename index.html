    So, what is cloud?

    “Jeff Bezos has likened Amazon Web Services to the utility companies of the early 1900s. One hundred years ago, a factory needing electricity would build its own power plant but, once the factories were able to buy electricity from a public utility, the need for pricey private electric plants subsided. AWS is trying to move companies away from physical computing technology and onto the cloud.” investopedia- What Is Amazon Web Services and Why Is It So Successful?. My anecdotally informed impression is that computing and storage are expensive to scale along with your customer base. As a company, your options are basically stop growing, build the infrastructure yourself (expensive), or rent the infrastructure from someone else (less expensive... most of the time). Data centers are the service that caters to that third option. But what is cloud? Clouds are what happens when the biggest, baddest data centers eat all the other data centers, and everyone goes to them if they can't afford to make their own infrastructure. It's when remote computing and remote storage are provided to anyone with money and an internet connection. When I was in primary school, we got OneDrive, suddenly your schoolwork was available no matter what computer you logged into, because instead of being saved to an onboard hard drive you were saving it to a “cloud” hosted on, or across warehouses full of racks of computers probably halfway around the world. But OneDrive isn't “the cloud” it’s a service Microsoft provides. The cloud is the name given to remote computing as a whole. That cloud of invisible, centralized power, hosted on a bunch of data centers scattered around the world, is what we call, the “cloud” what are services and servers? “As of the first quarter of 2021, one independent analyst reports AWS has over a third of the market at 32.4%, with Azure following behind at 20%, and Google Cloud at 9%.” Investopedia- What Is Amazon Web Services and Why Is It So Successful? Right now, AWS and Azure (Microsoft) are slugging it out for market control, but aside from the percentage of the worlds data centers that they own, how do they have control? Services and servers is the answer. Services are products that these companies provide, like ms teams, but also more backend things, like the amazon API gateway. Services are like subdivisions of what AWS or Azure could provide as one package, they divide like this to allow customers to pay only for what they want, and so they can charge as much as possible for what they do provide. What are servers? If you googled it you would get “a computer or computer program which manages access to a centralized resource or service in a network.” but in a cloud setting they are more like slices of computing power. The key thing about cloud computing is it's not central to one location, it is access to computing power hosted from all over the world. So what companies do is they go out and sell access to that pool, that access is a “server” hence the name it “serves you”. That being said, I think usually what people mean when they say “server” is the computing and memory itself. So to conclude, services are products that service providers like AWS and Azure provide, they either augment or enable certain features a customer wants, or they are fully developed applications like ms teams. Servers are slices of the pool of computing power that cloud providers have, together with the “services” they form a blank slate of an online company.

    Cybersecurity

    What does it do? Cyber security is defined as the practice of defending computers, servers, networks, all types of IT devices and data from malicious attacks. These attacks generally fall into either; phishing, malware, ransom ware and social engineering. The more successful attacks will most likely use a combination of these 4 types. In essence cyber security in a business setting is all about reducing total costs when the eventuality of a cyberattack occurs. There is a constant battle between cyber security professionals and the threat/bad actors (cyber attackers). Is the company better protected and better prepared or has a new malware been created or vulnerability been found that may be the key in breaching the company's defenses? The methods and technologies that threat actors use is constantly evolving and so must the methods and technologies that companies use to protect themselves evolve in the same way. An example of an attack is: A vulnerability is found in a newly updated software a company is running, which allows unauthorized access through the network. A black hat hacker targets this company because they are using this software that has this vulnerability. The hacker breaches the company network and has access to customer information. The company becomes aware of the attack when some data is accessed and stollen. The company contacts their cyber security operation center which begins their incident response procedure. This will take many days and will cost the company in loss revenue and loss in trust by their customers as their data was stollen. In the end the attack has had a huge impact on the company which may never recover. This shows how important cyber security is. The average cost to a larger company may be in the millions and the cost is increasing. “Data breach average cost increased 2.6% from USD 4.24 million in 2021 to USD 4.35 million in 2022. The average cost has climbed 12.7% from USD 3.86 million in the 2020 report.” This report also shows that during the pandemic there was a much greater growth in cost to companies. https://www.ibm.com/au-en/security/data-breach “Future conflict will involve sophisticated cyber warfare. Nations across the globe have recognized the strategic value and asymmetric advantage of investment in offensive cyber capabilities.” In the future, cyber security will be a major contributor in how conflicts and even wars play out. Australia and many other countries recognize this and have started to strengthen their cyber security in their defense sectors. In the future, wars may end before even 1 bullet, or one life is lost as the impact that a state sponsored cyberattack can have on a countries infrastructure and resources leads to ending the conflict before it has even begun. https://www.defence.gov.au/about/strategic-planning/defence-cyber-security-strategy

    What is machine learning

    In the modern day, machine learning is the invisible technology that surrounds us. It is always there behind the scenes processing data. But what is it exactly? Essentially machine learning is a process in which a computer is fed a series of instructions and then set to work to identify and categorize data. An example of machine learning we see in our everyday lives is on Spotify. When we listen to a song on Spotify, the application will take that information and then supply the user with other songs that are similar. We, the user, give the machine information about what we enjoy, and it is then able to process it and suggest other songs for us to listen to. There are three main machine learning algorithms that see wide use in the present, with a fourth gaining popularity. Supervised Machine Learning Unsupervised Machine Learning Reinforcement Learning These each have their own benefits and disadvantages as well as different uses in a variety of contexts but will continue to see usage for years to come. Supervised Machine Learning is an algorithm where we input information that the program can then use to identify and categorize future inputs. For example, if we were to feed the program hundreds of images of cats and dogs, with each image being labelled, the algorithm would ideally be able to understand aspects of what makes a cat a cat, versus a dog. It will start looking for several aspects, like the shape of the eyes, ears, body and tail, the algorithm will then categorize it as either a cat or a dog. We commonly see this algorithm utilized in fraudulent email protection, determining a loan applicant’s viability and detecting fraudulent bank withdrawals. Unsupervised Machine Learning is a somewhat messier approach. Essentially instead of teaching the algorithm what the information we are putting into it is, we will instead just allow the program to arbitrarily sort through the information and decide how to categorize it. This is useful to identify otherwise hidden patterns in datasets. This is widely seen in identifying customer’s behaviour, grouping it with other customers and recommending products based on the buying behaviour of other customers in the same group. Reinforcement Learning is most closely related to how humans learn. Instead of being fed pre-labelled datasets, a machine running the algorithm is instead allowed to interact with its environment. The machine is then rewarded for its good behaviour and punished for its bad behaviour. Over time it is expected the machine can determine what it should do as opposed to what it shouldn’t do. This algorithm is often seen in robotics, self-driving cars and AI designed to play games. Finally, a fourth algorithm is not fully embraced by everyone but is set to see more use in coming years. Semi-supervised Learning is a hybrid of supervised learning and unsupervised learning, hence the name. The algorithm is fed a small portion of labelled data, as well as a larger amount of unlabelled data, using this data it can sort the data to a high degree. Semi-supervised learning is still considered to be relatively new compared to the other methods but has already proven effective to minimize the issues that arise when using the other methods. These algorithms are all seeing constant use presently but there are new innovations on the horizon, as with most cutting-edge technologies. Automated Machine Learning is already seeing some use but is expected to become much more refined in the near future.
